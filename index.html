ML Route Optimization Engine — Event-Aware Dynamic Re-sequencing

Led product definition and cross-functional coordination for a machine learning-driven route optimization engine covering FedEx pickup and delivery operations across the US and Canada. The core problem: on-call pickups were historically appended to the end of driver routes regardless of their geographic position — creating unnecessary distance, fuel cost, and delivery delays. The ML engine replaced this with dynamic re-sequencing, inserting each new pickup into the optimal position in the existing route based on geolocation proximity and time-window constraints of already-committed stops.

A core architectural decision I championed was making the system event-aware from the ground up — not a static optimizer, but one that could ingest any structured external disruption signal and translate it into proactive route adjustments before disruption occurred. The engine recognised three disruption categories: scheduled sporting and civic events (predictable congestion zones with known time windows), demand surge events (volume spikes concentrated in specific zip codes), and infrastructure constraints (permit-based closures, road works). This made the system reusable across the annual event calendar without retraining the model for each new event.

Events integrated into production:

  • FIFA World Cup 2026 — Published match schedules ingested for all US host cities (Dallas, LA, NYC, Miami, Seattle, Boston, Philadelphia, Kansas City, San Francisco). Pickups within a defined radius of each stadium were automatically redistributed to non-congestion time windows, and reassigned to drivers positioned outside the expected congestion zone ahead of each match
  • Super Bowl LIX — New Orleans, Feb 2025 — French Quarter and Superdome-adjacent routes restructured for the full week prior to game day. Pickup density in high-footfall zones was shifted to early morning windows (before 9am) to execute before street closures and security perimeters activated — reducing rerouting exceptions on game day by eliminating the problem upstream
  • Thanksgiving / Black Friday — Nationwide surge signal built from 3 years of historical volume data and real-time retail partner demand forecasts. The engine dynamically rebalanced residential pickup density across fulfilment-heavy zip codes in the 72-hour pre-holiday window, pre-positioning driver capacity ahead of the spike
  • Mardi Gras — New Orleans, March 2025 — Multi-week street closure schedule ingested directly from city permit data via a structured feed. Affected route segments were automatically flagged and rerouted from the day the closure calendar was published — weeks in advance rather than reactively on the day
~30% route cost reduction 10–15% on-time improvement Event-aware ML US & Canada 3 disruption signal types Schedule-agnostic architecture
